1. Hardcore Native Push Notifications (Crucial)
The Problem: Right now, your voice alerts ("Fire! Fire!") only play when your app is actively open on the screen because
 we use WebSockets.
The Upgrade: Integrate Expo Push Notifications. If an alert triggers while the phone is locked in your pocket at 3 AM, 
your backend will send a high-priority push notification that wakes up the phone and makes a loud noise natively.

2. Live Historical Charts (Data Analytics)
The Upgrade: Stop just showing the current temperature. We can connect a database (like MongoDB or Supabase) to your
 backend to save every reading.
In the App: We build a second tab called "Analytics" featuring beautiful, interactive line charts (react-native-gifted-
charts). You can see exactly what time your kitchen gets the hottest, or if there are tiny, non-critical gas leaks happening 
every night at 8 PM.

3. Emergency SOS & Twilio SMS Integration
The Upgrade: Add a settings page in the app where you can save "Emergency Contacts".
How it works: If flame === 1, your backend automatically uses the Twilio API to fire off an SMS saying: 
"EMERGENCY: Fire detected at Aditya's ESP32 monitor. Please check immediately!" to those phone numbers. 
You can also add a massive red "Call Emergency Services" button directly beneath the alert card.

4. Remote Threshold Tuning
The Upgrade: Right now, the backend hardcodes gasAnalog > 1200. We can add sliders in your app settings to let
 you customize sensitivity remotely!
How it works: Drag the "Gas Sensitivity" slider in the app down to 800. The app sends the new rule to the backend, 
and the backend dynamically updates its monitoring rules without you ever having to re-flash the ESP32.

5. Smart "Device Offline" Detection
The Upgrade: What if the ESP32 loses power or the Wi-Fi drops, and a fire starts right after? You would never know.
How it works: The backend expects a heartbeat every 2 seconds. If 10 seconds pass with no HTTP POST, the backend emits 
out a "device_offline" socket event. The app's green pulsing dot turns grey, and it warns you: "Connection to sensor lost. 
Please check power."

6. Multi-Room Dashboard
The Upgrade: Right now it says Device: ESP32-01. If you buy a second ESP32, you can put one in the Kitchen and one in 
the Bedroom. We can upgrade the UI dashboard to show "Cards" for each room, allowing you to monitor your entire house
 simultaneously.
